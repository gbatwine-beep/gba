Liang Huanming Duan Ink Art Museum 梁煥明端硯藝術博物館

In ancient times, Zhaoqing was known as Duanzhou (端州). Therefore, inkstones produced in Zhaoqing are called "Duan inkstones" (端硯). As the top of China's four renowned inkstones, the Duan inkstones have a long history spanning over 2,000 years. Nowadays, the technique of producing Duan inkstones has been recognized as one of the national intangible cultural heritage items.

Liang Huanming (梁煥明), founder of the Liang Huanming Duan Inkstone Art Museum, is a master craftsman of inkstone artistry among China's Four Treasures of the Study (中國文房四寶). Liang also serves as a representative inheritor of the intangible cultural heritage "Duan Inkstone Craftsmanship".

As a comprehensive museum dedicated to showcasing the culture and artistry of Duan inkstones, the museum collects a vast number of valuable Duan inkstone pieces, related historical artifacts, and displays of craftsmanship techniques. Recently, the museum added an AR digital interactive experience, which allows participants to gain deeper insights into the historical background and cultural value of Duan inkstones through an interactive approach. The action of transforming the static display of artifacts into a dynamic interactive experience also attracted visitors of all ages to visit the museum.
