Shunde Qinghui Garden 佛山順德清暉園

Shunde Qinghui Garden (順德清暉園), located in Shunde District of Foshan, is one of the most famous classical gardens in Guangdong province. It is one of the Four Great Gardens of Guangdong (廣東四大名園) in China. Shunde Qinghui Garden has a rich history that reflects the cultural and architectural heritage of the region.

Shunde Qinghui Garden was established during the Qing Dynasty (清朝). It exemplifies traditional Lingnan garden design (嶺南園林設計), featuring picturesque landscaping, pavilions (展館), water and rockeries (山水) with scenic views at every turn in the pathway. The garden has been has been well-preserved over the centuries and recognized as a significant cultural site, preserving the architectural and aesthetic qualities of its time.

Over the years, Qinghui Garden served as a cultural hub (文化中心) where local scholars and artists gathered, fostering creativity and intellectual exchange. It is classified as a "Cultural Relic Protection Unit" in China (全國重點文物保護單位), which highlights its significance as a historical and cultural site. This classification underscores its importance in the preservation of cultural heritage and history.

Shunde Qinghui Garden is also recognized as a National AAAA Tourist Attraction (國家AAAA級旅遊景區), attracting visitors from around the world who come to admire its beauty and learn about its historical significance. The garden continues to be a testament to the rich cultural heritage of the Lingnan region and a cherished landmark in Guangdong province.
