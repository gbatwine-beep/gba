B. Victoria Peak (太平山)

Victoria Peak, locally known simply as "The Peak" (山頂), is Hong Kong's premier vantage point. At 552 meters above sea level, it offers the best panoramic views of Victoria Harbour. Developed during the 19th-century British colonial era (19世紀英國殖民時期) as a summer retreat, the Peak remains a must-visit destination.

The Peak Tower (山頂凌霄閣), an architectural marvel, showcases cutting-edge design and houses attractions such as Madame Tussauds Hong Kong (香港杜莎夫人蠟像館) and Peak Tram Historical Gallery (山頂纜車歷史珍藏館). Visitors can also ascend to Sky Terrace 428 (凌霄閣摩天台428)—the highest 360° viewing platform in Hong Kong at 428 meters above sea level—for unparalleled day and night vistas.

The Peak Tram (山頂纜車), which opened in 1888, connects the Peak and Central. With its unique 45-degree tilt design, it is one of the oldest cable car systems in the world and still maintains a record of safe operation. Many tourists take the Peak Tram to the Peak. In addition, we can also take buses, minibuses, and taxis to the Peak.

Hong Kong's unique charm lies in its perfect combination of the tranquility of Victoria Peak and the vitality of Victoria Harbour. This dual quality also reflects Hong Kong's role in the GBA (大灣區) - both a thoughtful global financial center and an engine of innovation. As the GBA continues to develop, Hong Kong will continue to play an indispensable role as a bridge connecting China and the world with its unique legacy and forward-looking vision.
