Liang Qichao's Former Residence （梁啟超故居）

Liang Qichao's Former Residence (梁啟超故居) is situated in the Xinhui District (新會) of Jiangmen. Liang Qichao's Former Residence was built in 1890, which served as his residence during his later years and is now a museum dedicated to his life and work.

Liang Qichao was a leading advocate of the Hundred Days' Reform (百日維新) in 1898, which aimed to modernize China's political and educational systems. He authored numerous articles and books to address social, political, and cultural issues. He promoted the democratic idea of a modern China. Liang Qichao is the founder of influential newspapers such as "The People's Newspaper" (民報), which disseminated reformist ideas and critiques of the Qing government (清政府).

Liang Qichao's Former Residence features a blend of Chinese and Western architectural styles, reflecting the cultural exchanges of the late Qing Dynasty.

A huge restoration in the site was seen to maintain its architectural integrity and preserve artifacts that reflect Liang's life and contributions. In addition, the residence often hosts events, lectures, and exhibitions to educate visitors about Liang Qichao's impact on Chinese society and political thought.
