Birds' Paradise （小鳥天堂）

The Birds' Paradise (小鳥天堂) was established in 1995. It has been developed as a renowned eco-tourism destination (生態旅遊目的地) located in Jiangmen City, focusing on bird conservation (鳥類保護) and providing a natural habitat for various bird species. The Birds' Paradise is also a memorial park dedicated to the esteemed Chinese writer Ba Jin (巴金), who drew inspiration from this breathtaking scenery filled with trees and countless birds for his famous Chinese literature - "Bird's Paradise".

This scenic spot includes lush greenery, wetlands, and lakes, creating a picturesque setting for visitors. In 2021, Bird Paradise was successfully awarded the title of "National Wetland Park" (國家濕地公園). The paradise provides opportunities to see a variety of birds in their natural setting. This stunning scenery and diverse birdlife make it a popular spot for photographers.

In addition, there are some well-maintained walking trails and observation points inside the paradise, allowing visitors to have a closer look of various kinds of birds. In March 2023, the Birds' Paradise Scenic Area was designated as a national AAAA-level tourist attraction (國家AAAA級旅遊景區) by the Department of Culture and Tourism of Guangdong Province (廣東省文化和旅遊廳).

For educational purposes, the Birds' Paradise regularly offers educational activities to promote awareness about wildlife conservation (野生動物保護) and the importance of protecting natural habitats. Visitors can also enjoy the natural beauty and learn about Ba Jin's impact on Chinese literature, creating a unique blend of nature and culture.
