Macau 澳門

Macau is a key city in the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macau Greater Bay Area (GBA) (粵港澳大灣區), known as the "9+2" region ("9+2"區域). Due to Macau's unique historical status, its positioning in the GBA encompasses "World Center of Tourism and Leisure" (世界旅遊休閒中心), "Commercial and Trade Cooperation Service Platform between China and Portuguese-speaking Countries" (中國與葡語國家商貿合作服務平台), and "Exchange and Cooperation Base with Chinese Culture as the Mainstream and Diverse Cultures in Coexistence" (以中華文化為主流，多元文化共存的交流合作基地).

Renowned worldwide for its unique historical background and multicultural heritage, Macau stands as a shining testament to the harmonious blend of Eastern and Western civilizations. Macau developed a distinctive urban landscape and culinary culture during its 400-year Portuguese colonial history (1557-1999) (400年葡萄牙殖民歷史). Though the city returned to Chinese sovereignty as a Special Administrative Region in 1999, it has preserved its profound Portuguese charm while evolving into a fascinating cultural mosaic. Local gastronomy serves as the most delicious embodiment of this remarkable history.

Local gastronomy serves as the most delicious embodiment of this remarkable history. The fusion of Portuguese and Chinese culinary traditions has created a unique Macanese cuisine (澳門土生葡菜) that is celebrated worldwide. From the iconic Portuguese egg tarts (葡式蛋撻) to the complex flavors of African chicken (非洲雞), Macau's food scene offers a delightful journey through its multicultural heritage.
