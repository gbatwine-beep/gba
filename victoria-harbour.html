A. Victoria Harbour (維多利亞港)

Victoria Harbour is one of the most distinguished natural harbours in the world, serving as a stunning centerpiece for Hong Kong's skyline. It also earns a nickname "Pearl of the Orient" (東方之珠), underscoring its enduring role as a global metropolis in driving the city's economy and tourism.

In addition, Victoria Harbour's transportation network is extensive, with the feature of historic Star Ferry, three cross-harbor tunnels, and an extensive ferry network connecting multiple piers across Hong Kong Island and Kowloon. Spanning 41.88 square kilometers with a maximum depth of 14.5 meters, its superior geographical conditions also lead it to be one of the world's busiest container ports.

Victoria Harbour is surrounded by iconic landmarks, including the Hong Kong Convention and Exhibition Centre (its distinctive wing-shaped extension symbolizes Hong Kong's handover in 1997), the Central Ferris Wheel (a 60-meter-tall observation wheel offering breathtaking views of the harbour and Avenue of Stars (a waterfront promenade celebrating Hong Kong's cinematic heritage).

"A Symphony of Lights" (幻彩詠香江)—the world's largest permanent light and sound show—transforms the harbor into a dazzling spectacle regularly showcasing at 8 in the evening. Synchronized across more than 40 skyscrapers with lasers, LED displays, and music, it encapsulates Hong Kong's vibrancy and creativity.
