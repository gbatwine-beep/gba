Wong Fei-Hung Memorial Hall 黃飛鴻紀念館

Wong Fei Hung (黃飛鴻) remains a symbol of Foshan's rich martial arts, heritage and cultural identity.

Wong Fei Hung (1847–1924), born in Foshan, was a renowned martial artist, physician and folk hero. He established a martial arts school in Foshan, where he trained many students, contributing to the spread of his mastery - Hung Gar (洪家) Kung Fu.

Wong Fei-Hung Memorial Hall dedicated to him was built in Foshan in 2001. This museum is dedicated to his legacy, showcasing his life, martial arts, and contributions to Chinese culture.

Wong Fei Hung is also credited with popularizing the lion dance (舞獅), an important cultural tradition in Chinese festivals. He is often depicted as a cultural icon in films, television series, and literature, embodying the virtues of bravery (勇敢), righteousness (正義), and compassion (惻隱之心).
