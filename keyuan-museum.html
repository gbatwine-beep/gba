B. Keyuan Museum 可園博物館

Keyuan (可園) is one of the Four classical gardens of Guangdong Province (廣東四大名園) from China's Qing Dynasty (清朝), originally constructed in 1850. Today, it operates as the Keyuan Museum and is recognized as one of the National Key Cultural Heritage Sites (全國重點文物保護單位).

In 2025, Keyuan Museum was ranked fourth among Guangdong's most innovative museums, highlighting its ability to showcase creativity while preserving tradition. The museum has successfully integrated technology with heritage, creating a unique experience that bridges the gap between historical preservation and modern innovation.

Keyuan Museum has incorporated AI elements and Augmented Reality (AR) technology (擴增實境技術) to enhance visitor experiences. Interactive features such as educational games and digital reconstructions of architectural elements allow visitors to witness the fascinating collision of traditional design and modern technology.

The museum stands as a testament to how technology can enrich our understanding and appreciation of cultural heritage, making it a must-visit destination for those interested in the interplay of history and innovation.
