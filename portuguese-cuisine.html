A. Portuguese Cuisine and Macanese Cuisine (葡國菜與土生葡菜)

Portuguese and Macanese cuisines (葡國菜與土生葡菜) are generally divided into starters, main courses, and desserts, though most restaurants serve starters and main courses simultaneously. As a former Portuguese colony (前葡萄牙殖民地), Macau offers authentic Portuguese cuisine (正宗葡國菜) that leaves a lasting impression.

Macanese cuisine (土生葡菜) builds upon Portuguese culinary traditions while incorporating Chinese influences, using seasonal ingredients and spices from Europe, South America, Africa, and Southeast Asia. Its unique flavors (獨特的風味) are simply irresistible. In addition, Macanese cuisine was included in the list of Macau's intangible cultural heritage (非物質文化遺產) in 2012 and is protected and promoted by government agencies.

The signature dishes (招牌菜) in Macau show a vibrant fusion of flavors, including Bacalhau balls (馬介休球), curry crab (咖哩炒蟹), Portuguese seafood rice (葡式海鮮飯), and Portuguese egg tarts (葡撻). These dishes showcase a harmonious blend of ingredients, combining the traditional cooking methods with influences from Portuguese and Asian cuisines.

Bacalhau Balls (馬介休球)

Bacalhau balls (馬介休球) are fried croquettes made with mashed salted cod and potatoes. They are a must-order starter (必點前菜) for both locals and visitors.

Curry Crab (咖哩炒蟹)

Curry crab (咖哩炒蟹) prepared with whole green crabs is one of the more premium dishes (高級菜餚) in Macanese and Portuguese cuisine. This dish shows strong Indian influences (印度影響), in which distinctive spices blend perfectly with creamy coconut milk for an exceptional flavor.

Portuguese Seafood Rice (葡式海鮮飯)

Portuguese seafood rice (葡式海鮮飯) combines raw rice with fresh tomato puree, prawns, mussels, octopus, and other seafood. Different restaurants use two main cooking methods (烹飪方法): Some use refreshing tomato-based broth, while others prefer richer cream-based versions.

Portuguese Egg Tarts (葡撻)

Portuguese egg tarts (葡撻) were first created in the 19th century by nuns at a Portuguese monastery. After being introduced to Macau, they became wildly popular (極受歡迎). The rich custard filling with slightly bitter caramelized tops creates an utterly addictive treat.

These signature dishes (招牌菜) in Macau not only provide unique flavors to delight the palate, but also tell the story of the region's historical connections (歷史聯繫) and cultural exchanges (文化交流).
