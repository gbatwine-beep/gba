A. Guangzhou South Railway Station 廣州南站

Guangzhou South Railway Station (廣州南站) is one of the largest railway stations in Asia, covering an area of approximately 4.8 million square feet. It started its operation in 2010. It is considered a major hub for high-speed trains (高速列車), facilitating efficient travel across the region and beyond.

High-speed trains at Guangzhou South Railway Station can reach speeds of up to 350 km/h, greatly reducing travel times between major cities. This network connects Guangzhou with several key locations, including Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen, and Hong Kong. Additionally, the station is well-integrated with the Guangzhou Metro (廣州地鐵), providing easy access to the city.

The Guangzhou South Railway Station brings a significant economic impact in China. It is a crucial component of China's high-speed rail network (中國高速鐵路網) and accommodates millions of passengers annually. As a result, it plays a vital role in enhancing local and regional economies through its extensive connectivity across various regions of the country.
